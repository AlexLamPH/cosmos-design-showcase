LONGFORM

The Art of Slow Correspondence

WRITTEN BY ELIAS VANCE
October 14th, 1992

There is a peculiar weight to a physical letter that no digital transmission can replicate. It begins with the choice of paper—the tactile grain of linen or the smooth, industrial finish of a standard sheet. Then there is the ink, a fluid commitment that dries into the fibers.

"The post office is the only place where time still moves at the speed of a horse-drawn carriage, even if the mail travels by jet."

We find ourselves now at a crossroads where the archive is no longer a chest in an attic, but a fleeting sequence of bits in a cloud.
